# Breakwater Industry Audit and Implementation Advisory Plan

## 1. Purpose

Breakwater should offer industry-specific operational and AI audits that answer four practical questions:

1. Where is the organization losing time, money, quality, or strategic leverage?
2. Which problems are appropriate for process improvement, conventional software, automation, or AI?
3. What should the organization do now, defer, or avoid?
4. Who is qualified to implement each approved recommendation?

The service should preserve Breakwater's current position as an independent advisor. Breakwater diagnoses the business, evaluates options, defines the implementation requirements, and helps the client select and supervise delivery partners. It does not need to become a custom software development firm.

## 2. Positioning

### Core promise

> Breakwater turns industry context and operating reality into a prioritized, implementation-ready plan.

The result is not a generic AI report or a list of tools. It is an evidence-based set of decisions tied to business value, organizational readiness, risk, and a realistic delivery path.

### What Breakwater owns

- Business and operating-model assessment
- Industry and competitor context
- Workflow and process review
- AI, automation, software, and data-fit analysis
- Risk, governance, and readiness assessment
- Opportunity prioritization and business case
- Build, buy, configure, outsource, or defer recommendations
- Implementation brief development
- Provider evaluation and selection support
- Independent implementation oversight
- Post-implementation benefit review

### What Breakwater does not own

- Bespoke software or AI development
- Managed IT services
- Cybersecurity certification
- Legal, regulatory, or accounting opinions
- Reselling software for a commission
- Guaranteeing the performance of an outside provider

Specialists may perform those functions under separate contracts with the client.

## 4. Product Structure

Breakwater can package the work as a sequence rather than a single open-ended consulting engagement.

### Offer 1: Industry Opportunity Scan

**Purpose:** Give a leadership team a fast, grounded view of the most relevant developments in its industry.

**Typical duration:** 1-2 weeks

**Inputs:**

- Leadership interview
- Basic operating and financial context
- Current systems list
- Limited workflow sample
- Industry research

**Outputs:**

- Industry change summary
- Executive infographic summarizing the major industry forces, risks, and opportunities
- Relevant competitor and peer patterns
- Five to ten potential opportunities
- Major risks and constraints
- Recommended next step

This is a lower-cost entry product and a qualification step for a full audit.

### Offer 2: Operational and AI Audit

**Purpose:** Identify, evaluate, and prioritize concrete improvements across the organization.

**Typical duration:** 4-8 weeks, depending on scope

**Outputs:**

- Executive decision brief
- Current-state operating assessment
- Process and pain-point inventory
- Systems, data, and AI usage inventory
- Risk and readiness assessment
- Prioritized opportunity portfolio
- "Do now, do next, defer, and avoid" recommendations
- Preliminary economics and success measures
- Implementation pathway for each priority
- 90-day action plan and 12-month roadmap
- Presentation-ready visual summary of findings, priorities, and sequencing

### Offer 3: Provider Selection

**Purpose:** Convert an approved recommendation into a fair, comparable provider decision.

**Typical duration:** 2-5 weeks

**Outputs:**

- Implementation-ready statement of work
- Provider qualification criteria
- Request for information or request for proposal
- Vetted shortlist, normally two to four candidates
- Proposal comparison and interview support
- Reference-check framework
- Contract and milestone recommendations for client counsel to review
- Final selection memo

### Offer 4: Independent Implementation Oversight

**Purpose:** Protect the client's objectives after a provider has been selected.

**Typical duration:** Monthly through delivery

**Activities:**

- Kickoff and scope alignment
- Milestone and budget review
- Risk and issue review
- Acceptance-criteria validation
- Leadership reporting
- Change-management coordination
- Escalation support
- Benefit tracking

Breakwater should not direct the provider's personnel or assume delivery liability. Its role is owner-side advisory and quality oversight.

### Offer 5: Outcome Review

**Purpose:** Determine whether the implementation produced the promised result.

**Timing:** Approximately 60-120 days after launch, with later reviews for long-cycle benefits

**Outputs:**

- Baseline-to-current metric comparison
- Adoption and workflow review
- Financial and operational benefit assessment
- Risk or quality findings
- Optimization recommendations
- Continue, expand, repair, or stop decision

## 5. Audit Method

### Step 1: Frame the business question

Agree on the reason for the audit before evaluating tools.

- Strategic goals
- Material operating constraints
- Financial expectations
- Decisions the audit must support
- Included business units and processes
- Explicit exclusions
- Executive sponsor and working team

### Step 2: Establish an industry baseline

Assess the forces that should influence the client's choices:

- Customer expectations
- Labor and skill constraints
- Margin pressure
- Competitor behavior
- Technology adoption patterns
- Relevant regulation and contractual obligations
- Supply chain or market dependencies
- Practices that are becoming standard versus those that remain experimental

The baseline should distinguish verified capability from vendor claims.

### Step 3: Map the current operation

Review the workflows that create value or friction:

- Trigger and desired outcome
- People and roles involved
- Systems and data used
- Handoffs and approvals
- Cycle time and wait time
- Error, rework, and exception rates
- Customer impact
- Current cost
- Control and compliance requirements

Interviews should be supported by evidence such as sample documents, system reports, observed workflows, and available metrics.

### Step 4: Inventory technology, data, and AI use

Document:

- Core systems and owners
- Integration points
- Spreadsheets and manual workarounds
- Data sources, quality, access, and retention
- Current AI tools, approved and unapproved
- Software contracts and renewal dates
- Security and privacy controls
- Existing IT providers and internal capabilities

This prevents Breakwater from recommending a new product when the client already owns a sufficient capability.

### Step 5: Develop solution options

Each problem should be considered against multiple solution paths:

1. Stop or simplify the work.
2. Clarify policy, role, or process.
3. Improve use of an existing system.
4. Buy and configure a standard product.
5. Connect existing products with workflow automation.
6. Add a governed AI capability.
7. Commission a custom build.
8. Defer because readiness, economics, or risk is unfavorable.

AI should win only when it is better suited than the simpler alternatives.

### Step 6: Score opportunities

Use a consistent 1-5 scale and record the evidence behind each score.

| Dimension | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Strategic value | Does this advance a stated business priority? |
| Financial value | Is there credible revenue, margin, cost, or loss-avoidance value? |
| Customer or employee value | Does it materially improve an important experience? |
| Feasibility | Can available systems, data, and providers deliver it? |
| Readiness | Can the organization absorb and sustain the change? |
| Time to value | How quickly can measurable value appear? |
| Risk | What are the operational, legal, security, reputational, and model risks? |
| Evidence quality | How strong are the underlying data and assumptions? |

Recommendations should include the raw scores, rationale, dependencies, and confidence level. A numerical total should inform judgment, not replace it.

### Step 7: Build the roadmap

Organize recommendations into:

- **Do now:** High-confidence actions with manageable risk and clear ownership
- **Do next:** Valuable work that depends on a prerequisite
- **Pilot:** Uncertain but testable opportunities with a bounded downside
- **Defer:** Plausible opportunities that are not currently ready
- **Avoid:** Poor economics, unacceptable risk, or capability that does not fit the business

Every "do now," "do next," or "pilot" item needs an owner, budget range, metric, implementation path, and next decision date.

## 6. Recommendation Format

Each recommendation should fit a standard two-to-four-page decision record.

### Required fields

- Business problem
- Current-state evidence
- Affected users and workflows
- Recommended intervention
- Alternatives considered
- Why AI is or is not appropriate
- Expected value and calculation method
- One-time and recurring cost range
- Dependencies and prerequisites
- Material risks and controls
- Data and integration requirements
- Change-management implications
- Recommended implementation route
- Provider capabilities required
- Success metrics and baseline
- Decision owner
- Next action and decision date
- Breakwater confidence rating

This format gives the client a durable decision trail and gives prospective implementers enough clarity to estimate responsibly.

### Visual communication package

Breakwater should translate the audit's most important findings into a concise set of branded, presentation-ready visuals. These are communication deliverables, not substitutes for the underlying analysis.

Possible formats include:

- Industry forces and market-change infographic
- Current-state process or information-flow map
- Operational pain-point map
- Opportunity-versus-readiness matrix
- "Do now, do next, pilot, defer, avoid" portfolio
- Build, buy, configure, outsource, or defer decision tree
- Provider-category map showing who should implement each initiative
- 90-day and 12-month roadmap
- Executive scorecard with baseline and target measures
- One-page implementation brief for each approved priority

Each visual should have a defined audience and decision purpose. A board-facing summary may emphasize strategic exposure and investment decisions, while an operating-team visual may emphasize workflow, ownership, sequencing, and measures.

The standard audit package should include a limited number of visuals tied to the highest-value decisions. Additional industry briefings, conference graphics, client education materials, or campaign-ready infographic series can be scoped separately.

## 7. Determining Who Should Implement

Breakwater should recommend the provider category first, then the provider. This reduces the chance of forcing every problem toward a favored vendor.

### Implementation routes

| Route | Best used when | Typical implementer |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Internal process change | The problem is ownership, policy, or workflow discipline | Client's operating leader, with change support |
| Existing platform configuration | The client already owns the needed capability | Existing software vendor, certified partner, or internal administrator |
| Standard SaaS adoption | The need is common and products are mature | Product implementation team or specialist consultancy |
| Workflow automation | Stable systems need to exchange data or trigger routine actions | Automation/integration specialist |
| Data and reporting improvement | Decisions are blocked by fragmented or unreliable information | Data engineering or business-intelligence consultancy |
| Managed IT or security improvement | Infrastructure, identity, devices, or controls are weak | Managed service provider or cybersecurity firm |
| Governed AI deployment | A defined use case requires model integration, evaluation, monitoring, and controls | AI engineering firm with production and governance experience |
| Custom software | The workflow is strategically distinctive and standard products cannot meet it | Product development firm or qualified software engineering team |
| Training and adoption | The technology exists but behavior and capability lag | Change-management or role-specific training provider |
| Regulated-domain work | Specialized legal, privacy, safety, or compliance knowledge is material | Domain-qualified implementation and compliance specialists |

### Default decision order

Breakwater should generally test implementation options in this order:

1. Internal improvement
2. Better use of an existing system
3. Standard product
4. Integration or configuration
5. Custom development

Custom work should require a clear strategic reason, identifiable ownership, a credible maintenance plan, and economics that account for ongoing support.

### Provider intake record

Maintain a standard internal profile for each provider:

- Ownership and key personnel
- Service categories and industries
- Team size and delivery capacity
- Geographic coverage
- Typical project size
- Technical certifications
- Security and insurance posture
- Reference clients
- Sample work
- Pricing model
- Use of subcontractors
- Conflicts of interest
- Accessibility and support model
- Last review date
- Breakwater engagement history
- Outcome and client-feedback notes

### Qualification standards

A provider should not be described as vetted until Breakwater has:

- Interviewed the delivery lead, not only a salesperson
- Reviewed at least two relevant references
- Examined a representative work product or demonstration
- Confirmed who will perform the work
- Reviewed security and privacy practices appropriate to the engagement
- Confirmed professional and cyber insurance where relevant
- Evaluated project governance and change-control practices
- Assessed post-launch support
- Documented any financial or personal relationship

"Vetted" means evaluated against stated criteria. It should not be presented as a warranty.

## 9. Provider Selection Process

### 1. Write the implementation brief

The brief should define outcomes and constraints without prescribing unnecessary technical details.

- Problem and users
- In-scope and out-of-scope work
- Required capabilities
- Existing systems and data
- Security, privacy, and compliance requirements
- Deliverables
- Acceptance criteria
- Timeline and budget range
- Support expectations
- Proposal response format

### 2. Build the shortlist

Include two to four credible choices when the market allows. The shortlist may contain providers from Breakwater's network and qualified providers identified specifically for the engagement.

### 3. Score consistently

| Criterion | Suggested weight |
| --- | ---: |
| Relevant problem and industry experience | 20% |
| Proposed solution quality | 20% |
| Delivery team quality and availability | 15% |
| Security, privacy, and risk controls | 15% |
| Implementation and adoption plan | 10% |
| Total cost of ownership | 10% |
| Support and maintainability | 5% |
| References and demonstrated outcomes | 5% |

Weights should change when the engagement requires it. Regulated or high-impact work should place more weight on controls, evidence, and support.

### 4. Test the proposal

Breakwater should challenge:

- Assumptions hidden behind fixed prices
- Unspecified integrations or data-cleaning work
- Dependence on proprietary components
- Model and software usage charges
- Security responsibilities
- Ownership of data, prompts, configurations, and code
- Acceptance criteria
- Training and adoption effort
- Maintenance, monitoring, and exit costs
- Claims that cannot be tied to evidence

### 5. Recommend, but preserve client choice

The final memo should identify:

- Recommended provider
- Why it is the best fit
- Material tradeoffs
- Conditions to resolve before signing
- Runner-up and when it would be preferable
- Any unresolved uncertainty

The client should contract directly with the provider.

## 10. Independence and Ethics

Breakwater's credibility depends on separating advice from implementation economics.

### Recommended policy

- Do not accept undisclosed referral fees.
- Prefer no referral fees at all.
- Do not rank a provider more highly because of a partnership.
- Disclose personal, financial, and prior-client relationships.
- Give clients the scoring criteria and relevant evidence.
- Allow clients to introduce their own candidates.
- Do not share confidential client information without written authorization.
- Do not let an implementation provider write or control the audit conclusion.
- Use separate scopes and invoices for audit, selection, and oversight.
- State that legal, tax, compliance, and security certifications require qualified specialists.

If Breakwater ever accepts a fee from a provider, it should disclose the amount and obtain the client's written consent before making the introduction. The cleaner long-term model is for the client to pay Breakwater for independent selection and oversight.

## 11. Commercial Model

Pricing should reflect scope, complexity, evidence required, and risk. The figures below are planning ranges to validate against the market, not published commitments.

| Service | Planning range |
| --- | ---: |
| Industry Opportunity Scan | $3,000-$7,500 |
| Focused department or workflow audit | $7,500-$18,000 |
| Organization-wide operational and AI audit | $18,000-$50,000+ |
| Provider selection | $5,000-$15,000 or 10-15% of expected implementation cost, with a cap |
| Implementation oversight | $2,500-$8,000 per month |
| Outcome review | $3,000-$10,000 |

### Pricing principles

- Use fixed fees when scope and access are clear.
- Use a paid discovery phase when scope is uncertain.
- Tie payment to Breakwater deliverables, not the provider's sales or project value.
- Define interview, process, location, and recommendation limits.
- Price regulated, multi-location, or data-intensive work separately.
- Require a deposit and milestone payments.
- Put additional work through written change control.

## 14. Quality Standards

Every final audit should meet the following minimum standard:

- Important claims cite a source, interview, observation, or client dataset.
- Facts, assumptions, estimates, and judgments are labeled separately.
- Recommendations include alternatives and a "do nothing" comparison.
- Financial benefits show the calculation and confidence level.
- Costs include implementation, licenses, integration, training, support, and internal time.
- AI recommendations address evaluation, human review, failure modes, privacy, security, and monitoring.
- Provider recommendations disclose conflicts and use documented criteria.
- The executive report can be understood without technical expertise.
- Every infographic has a specific decision purpose and cites its underlying sources.
- Visuals distinguish verified facts, client data, estimates, and Breakwater judgments.
- Charts preserve scale and context and do not imply unsupported precision.
- Client-confidential information is excluded from public or reusable graphics unless explicitly authorized.
- The client reviews names, figures, and sensitive claims before any visual is published externally.
- Final visuals are delivered in presentation-ready and accessible digital formats.
- The implementation brief is detailed enough for comparable proposals.
- The client receives editable copies of its roadmap and requirements.

## 16. Launch Plan

### First 30 days: define the service

- Select two Phase 1 industry audit variants.
- Define the minimum audit scope and exclusions.
- Create the core interview, inventory, scoring, report, and implementation-brief templates.
- Define a branded visual system for audit infographics, scorecards, process maps, and roadmaps.
- Write the independence and conflict-of-interest policy.
- Identify ten to fifteen provider categories and initial candidates.
- Create a one-page service description and sample deliverables.

### Days 31-60: test the method

- Recruit one or two pilot clients at a clearly stated pilot price.
- Run the full method and record actual hours.
- Have an outside specialist review the security, privacy, and contracting language.
- Interview prospective implementation providers.
- Refine scoring weights and report structure.
- Produce an anonymized sample recommendation.
- Produce an anonymized sample industry infographic and visual roadmap.

### Days 61-90: package and sell

- Publish the industry audit offer on the website.
- Add a qualification form focused on business problem, industry, size, systems, and urgency.
- Publish one substantive industry briefing.
- Formalize the initial provider directory.
- Establish fixed starting prices or minimum engagement levels.
- Create a quarterly follow-up process for audit clients.

### Months 4-12: build evidence

- Complete five to ten audits in the initial verticals.
- Track recommendation and implementation outcomes.
- Develop anonymized case studies with measurable results.
- Remove providers who do not meet delivery standards.
- Add a new vertical only after the existing method is repeatable.
- Consider an annual industry benchmark report using aggregated, anonymized findings.

## 18. Recommended Initial Model

The strongest initial model is:

1. Start with retail/consumer and supply chain/distribution audits.
2. Sell a focused opportunity scan as the entry point.
3. Convert qualified clients into a fixed-scope operational and AI audit.
4. Deliver a prioritized roadmap and implementation-ready briefs.
5. Offer a separate, paid provider-selection engagement.
6. Have the client contract directly with the implementer.
7. Offer monthly owner-side oversight through acceptance and adoption.
8. Return after launch to verify outcomes.
9. Take no provider commissions.
10. Expand industries only after the method and provider network have demonstrated results.

This model turns Breakwater's "consulting only" position into a commercial advantage: the team can tell clients what to build, buy, improve, or avoid without needing to sell the implementation itself.
