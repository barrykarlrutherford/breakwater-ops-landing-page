Industry Audit & Implementation Advisory

Move From General Possibilities to Specific Decisions

Breakwater examines the business, its industry, and its operating reality before recommending technology. Every audit ends with a prioritized plan, practical economics, and a clear path to implementation.

What Breakwater Examines

  • Industry pressure: Customer expectations, competitors, labor constraints, margin pressure, and relevant technology shifts
  • Operating workflows: Delays, handoffs, rework, exceptions, and decisions that consume disproportionate time
  • Systems and data: Existing platforms, spreadsheets, integrations, data quality, contracts, and unused capabilities
  • Current AI use: Approved tools, informal usage, measurable value, duplication, cost, and risk
  • Organizational readiness: Ownership, skills, security, governance, adoption capacity, and change constraints

What Leadership Receives

  • Executive decision brief with the most important findings and choices
  • Prioritized opportunity portfolio organized as do now, do next, pilot, defer, and avoid
  • Business cases with expected value, cost range, dependencies, risk, and confidence
  • Implementation-ready briefs that qualified providers can estimate against
  • 90-day and 12-month roadmaps with owners, measures, and decision dates
  • Presentation-ready visuals that make findings and sequencing clear to boards, leaders, and operating teams

From Recommendation to Delivery

Know What to Do and Who Should Do It

Breakwater recommends the right implementation route before recommending a provider. The answer may be an internal process change, better use of an existing platform, a standard product, an integration specialist, or a qualified custom development team.

01

Improve Internally

When the real issue is policy, ownership, workflow discipline, or better use of tools already in place.

02

Configure or Buy

When an existing platform or mature standard product can meet the requirement without custom complexity.

03

Use a Specialist

For integration, automation, data, security, managed IT, governed AI, training, or regulated work.

04

Build Selectively

Only when the workflow is strategically distinctive and ownership, maintenance, risk, and economics are clear.

Independent Provider Selection

For approved initiatives, Breakwater can write the implementation brief, identify the required capabilities, build a qualified shortlist, compare proposals, check references, and document the tradeoffs behind a final recommendation.

Owner-Side Oversight

After selection, Breakwater can review milestones, budget, risk, acceptance criteria, adoption, and measured outcomes without taking over the provider's delivery responsibility.

The principle is simple: clients pay Breakwater for independent judgment. Breakwater does not need to sell the software or perform the build to recommend the right answer.

Breakwater's Approach

Strategy Before Tools

Most AI consultants start with technology. Breakwater starts with the business. What are the actual constraints? Where do people waste time? What decisions take too long?

Only after understanding operations does Breakwater consider whether the answer is process improvement, existing software, automation, AI, outside expertise, or no new technology at all.

Honest Counsel Over Hype

AI is not magic. It's a tool. Sometimes it's the right tool. Often it's not. Barry Rutherford's fifty years of operating experience and Griffin Rutherford's technical AI research background create a clear filter for the difference between genuine capability and expensive complexity.

Consulting Only

Breakwater provides strategy, audit, provider selection, training, and owner-side advisory support. It does not sell bespoke AI builds or proprietary automation packages, allowing its recommendations to remain independent.

Core Principles

  • Assess first: Understand where you are before recommending where to go
  • Human-centered: AI augments people, doesn't replace judgment
  • Measurable wins: Clear ROI, not vague promises
  • Implementation-ready: Every priority identifies ownership, economics, risk, measures, and the right delivery route
  • Specific operating judgment: Advice draws from supply chain, retail, luxury brand, and global market experience
  • Change management: Technology without adoption is worthless
