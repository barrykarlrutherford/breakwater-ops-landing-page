# Breakwater Industry Landing Page Variations

## Purpose

This document defines 25 industry-specific landing-page concepts for Breakwater Operations. Each variation adapts the same core offer:

> Breakwater examines the operation before recommending technology, identifies what is worth fixing, and defines who should implement it.

These are page briefs, not finished pages. They are designed to support copywriting, design, SEO planning, campaign testing, and phased implementation without changing Breakwater's independence or service model.

## Shared Page Structure

Every industry page should use the same underlying structure so the site remains maintainable:

1. Industry-specific hero and primary call to action
2. Four recognizable operating pressures
3. Breakwater's combined business and technical assessment
4. Four practical outcomes
5. Industry-specific decision principles or leadership questions
6. Three offers: Opportunity Scan, Operational and AI Audit, Selection and Oversight
7. Consultation section
8. Appropriate legal, privacy, safety, or compliance caveat

Do not imply that Breakwater provides legal advice, regulated professional advice, cybersecurity certification, custom software development, or guaranteed financial results.

## Implementation Guidance

### Build one reusable template

Do not create 25 unrelated page designs. Extend the structure already used by `nonprofits.html` into a data-driven or consistently templated page system. Each page should vary through:

- Metadata
- Hero copy
- Industry pain points
- Outcome labels
- Decision questions
- Offer name
- CTA wording
- Specialist caveat

Navigation, service architecture, team positioning, consultation flow, footer, responsive behavior, accessibility, and analytics should remain shared.

### Publish in controlled batches

Recommended first batch:

1. Retail and ecommerce
2. Wholesale distribution
3. Professional services
4. Hotels and lodging
5. Nonprofits

Recommended second batch:

1. Luxury and premium brands
2. Light manufacturing
3. Tourism and attractions
4. Construction
5. Skilled trades and home services

Publish specialist-supported pages only after their claims, caveats, intake questions, and referral model have been reviewed by appropriate domain experts.

### Avoid thin SEO pages

Each published page should include more than substituted industry nouns. Before launch, add:

- One original industry insight
- One industry-specific workflow diagram or visual
- A short description of what Breakwater examines
- A relevant sample deliverable
- Three to five qualification questions
- A distinct FAQ
- A clear explanation of domain boundaries

### Track page performance

Measure:

- Qualified consultation requests by page
- CTA click-through rate
- Consultation booking completion
- Engagement with pain-point and service sections
- Search impressions and qualified organic traffic
- Lead quality by industry
- Opportunity Scan conversion rate
- Audit conversion rate

Do not judge a page only by traffic. A lower-volume page that produces credible, well-matched engagements is more valuable than a broad page that attracts buyers seeking software development or generic AI training.
